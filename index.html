I am a PhD candidate at the University of Oviedo (UNIOVI) working on plant epigenomics and quantitative histone proteomics. My main focus is to understand how histone post-translational modifications (hPTMs) encode developmental and stress-related information in plants, with H3K79 as a central motif.

Alongside the experimental work, I design and maintain an ecosystem of reproducible tools ‚Äì from WIFF/mzML conversion and QC to histone peptide quantification and downstream statistical analyses ‚Äì aiming to make plant epiproteomics transparent, auditable and reusable.

This site will progressively host my thesis materials, open datasets, code repositories, teaching resources and a blog-style lab notebook.

EpiProfile_PLANTS ecosystem
From WIFF files to histone PTM matrices, QC and interpretation.

EpiProfile_PLANTS is a MATLAB-based suite for relative quantification of histone peptides from bottom-up proteomics data. The plant version adds:

  • Predefined histone catalogs for Arabidopsis, Marchantia, Chlamydomonas
  • Curated retention time windows for plant histone peptides
  • QC reports with acquisition and processing checks
  • Batch processing for multi-condition experiments
  • Export formats compatible with downstream R/Python analysis

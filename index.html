I am a PhD candidate at the University of Oviedo (UNIOVI) working on plant epigenomics and quantitative histone proteomics. My research focuses on how combinatorial histone marks change across development and senescence, using propionylation-based bottom-up LC‚ÄìMS/MS and conservative quantification strategies.

A core part of my work is making the computational layer explicit: what is assumed, what is measured, how uncertainty enters, and how results can be audited. In practice, this means reproducible conversion (vendor files ‚Üí mzML), standardized intermediate artifacts (MS1/MS2 extraction), and clear outputs for downstream analysis (hDP/hPF/hPTM matrices).

This website is a living index of my thesis-related projects, open-source code, reanalyses of public datasets and teaching material.

EpiProfile_PLANTS ecosystem
Plant-oriented EpiProfile 2.0 extension: quantification + QC + audit-ready outputs.

EpiProfile_PLANTS is a MATLAB-based suite for relative quantification of histone peptides and peptideforms from propionylation-based bottom-up proteomics. The key output layers are:

  • hDP: peptide backbones (sequence-level units)
  • hPF: peptideforms / PTM states (combinatorial forms per peptide)
  • hPTM: site-level summaries derived from hPF marginals

The plant extension focuses on curated species catalogs/layouts and on making the processing contract explicit: deterministic inputs, traceable intermediates (mzML ‚Üí MS1/MS2), and QC artifacts that help interpret failures (e.g., retention time drift, empty windows, layout mismatches).

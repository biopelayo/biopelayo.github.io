Bio
Computational biologist working at the intersection of plant epigenomics, proteomics and open workflows.

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Oviedo (UNIOVI) working on plant epigenomics and quantitative histone proteomics. My main focus is to understand how histone post-translational modifications (hPTMs) encode developmental and stress-related information in plants, with H3K79 as a central motif.

Alongside the experimental work, I design and maintain an ecosystem of reproducible tools ‚Äì from WIFF/mzML conversion and QC to histone peptide quantification and downstream statistical analyses ‚Äì aiming to make plant epiproteomics transparent, auditable and reusable.

This site will progressively host my thesis materials, open datasets, code repositories, teaching resources and a blog-style lab notebook.

EpiProfile_PLANTS ecosystem
From WIFF files to histone PTM matrices, QC and biological interpretation.

EpiProfile_PLANTS is my adaptation of EpiProfile 2.0 for plants, including species-specific histone definitions, curated retention-time windows and utilities to audit the full LC‚ÄìMS/MS acquisition chain.

The goal is to provide an end-to-end workflow that makes it easy to reproduce analyses from vendor WIFF/WIFFSCAN files all the way to figures in the thesis and associated papers, while keeping every step documented and versioned.

1. Conversion & pre-processing

Standardised conversion of WIFF/WIFFSCAN to mzML (profile/centroid), extraction of MS1/MS2 traces and storage of LC‚ÄìMS/MS acquisition reports as a core reproducibility layer.

ProteoWizard msconvert Docker / WSL2
2. Quantification with EpiProfile_PLANTS

Adapted MATLAB code with plant-specific layouts, histone peptide catalogs and panel-based quantification of hPTMs, including emerging marks on H3K79 in Arabidopsis.

GPL-3.0 Histone PTMs
3. Downstream analysis & QC

R-based pipelines for normalisation (qsmooth, ComBat), exploratory analysis, differential testing and visualisation, together with small demonstration datasets for teaching and methods papers.

R / Bioconductor QC dashboards

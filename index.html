About Me

Computational biologist working at the intersection of plant chromatin, proteomics and reproducible workflows.

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Oviedo (UNIOVI) working on plant epigenomics and quantitative histone proteomics. My research focuses on how combinatorial histone marks change across development and senescence, using propionylation-based bottom-up LC-MS/MS and conservative quantification strategies.

A core part of my work is making the computational layer explicit: what is assumed, what is measured, how uncertainty enters, and how results can be audited. In practice, this means reproducible conversion (vendor files to mzML), standardized intermediate artifacts (MS1/MS2 extraction), and clear outputs for downstream analysis (hDP/hPF/hPTM matrices).

I also contribute to cancer bioinformatics at the Centro Nacional de Investigaciones Oncológicas (CNIO), applying transcriptomics and genomics approaches to oncology research.

This website is a living index of my thesis-related projects, cancer bioinformatics work, open-source code, reanalyses of public datasets, and teaching material.

EpiProfile_PLANTS

Plant-oriented EpiProfile 2.0 extension: quantification + QC + audit-ready outputs.

EpiProfile_PLANTS is a MATLAB-based suite for relative quantification of histone peptides and peptideforms from propionylation-based bottom-up proteomics. The key output layers are:

hDP
Peptide backbones (sequence-level units)
hPF
Peptideforms / PTM states (combinatorial forms)
hPTM
Site-level summaries from hPF marginals

The plant extension focuses on curated species catalogs/layouts and on making the processing contract explicit: deterministic inputs, traceable intermediates (mzML to MS1/MS2), and QC artifacts that help interpret failures (e.g., retention time drift, empty windows, layout mismatches).
